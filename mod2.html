Understanding Quadrilaterals

Welcome to the module on quadrilaterals! In this module, you will learn about different types of quadrilaterals, their properties, and how to solve problems involving them. Quadrilaterals are four-sided polygons, and they come in various shapes and sizes. By the end of this module, you'll be able to identify and classify quadrilaterals, understand their properties, and solve basic problems related to them.

Objectives
Define and identify quadrilaterals.
Classify quadrilaterals based on their properties.
Understand the properties of special quadrilaterals: parallelograms, rectangles, squares, rhombuses, and trapezoids.

Introduction to Quadrilaterals

A quadrilateral is a polygon with four sides (edges) and four vertices (corners). The sum of the interior angles of any quadrilateral is always 360 degrees.



The figure show quadrilateral PQRS or ▭PQRS Quadrilaterals are name using the four vertices consecutively. The other names for ▭PQRS are as follows:
▭QRSP, ▭RSPQ, ▭SPQR, ▭PSRQ
▭SRQP, ▭RQPS, and ▭QPSR

The sides of ▭PQRS are PQ, QR, RS, and SP, Its vertices are P, Q, R and S and its angles are ∠P, ∠Q, ∠R, and ∠S. The diagonals are PR and QS

Solving Problems Involving Quadrilaterals

Example Problems

Given a quadrilateral with angles measuring 90°, 85°, and 95°,find fourth angle.

The sum of the interior angles of a quadrilateral is 360°.

Fourth angle=360°−(90°+85°+95°)=360°−270°=90°

Therefore, the fourth angle is 90°.

Example Problems

A rectangle has a length of 8 cm and a width of 6 cm. Find the length of the diagonals.

The diagonals of a rectangle are equal in length and can be found using the Pythagorean theorem.

Diagonal= square root of length² width²= √(8²+6²)= 10 cm

Therefore, the length of each diagonal is 10 cm.

    In this module, you've learned about the different types of quadrilaterals, their properties, and how to solve problems involving them. Understanding these fundamental shapes and their characteristics is essential in geometry. Keep practicing these concepts, and you'll be able to tackle more complex problems with confidence.

    1. Parallelogram

    A quadrilateral with opposite sides parallel and equal in length. Examples: Rhombus, rectangle, and square.

    2.Rectangle

    A parallelogram with four right angles. Properties: Opposite sides are equal and parallel, and diagonals are equal in length.

    3. Square

    A rectangle with all sides equal in length. Properties: All properties of a rectangle and a rhombus; four right angles and diagonals bisect each other at right angles.

    4. Rhombus

    A parallelogram with all four sides equal in length. Properties: Opposite angles are equal, diagonals bisect each other at right angles, and diagonals are not equal.

    5. Kite

    A quadrilateral with two pairs of adjacent sides equal. Properties: One pair of opposite angles is equal, and diagonals intersect at right angles with one diagonal bisected.

    6. Trapezoid (or Trapezium):

    Only one pair of opposite sides is parallel. Properties:Only one pair of opposite sides is parallel. Non-parallel sides are called legs.
