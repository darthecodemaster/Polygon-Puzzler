  • Types of Triangles
    • Types of Triangles
    Triangles, fundamental geometric shapes, are categorized by side lengths and angles. Equilateral triangles have equal sides; isosceles have two equal sides; scalene have none. Based on angles, they can be acute, right, or obtuse, defining their unique properties and applications:

    1. Scalene Triangle

    A scalene triangle is a type of triangle where all three sides and all three angles are of different lengths and degrees, respectively. This distinct lack of symmetry differentiates it from equilateral and isosceles triangles.

    2. Isosceles

    An isosceles triangle is a triangle with at least two sides of equal length, resulting in two equal angles opposite those sides. This symmetry distinguishes it from scalene triangles, which have no equal sides, and equilateral triangles, with all sides equal.

    3. Equilateral Triangle

    An equilateral triangle is a triangle with all three sides of equal length and all three internal angles measuring 60 degrees each. This uniformity gives it perfect symmetry, distinguishing it from isosceles and scalene triangles, which lack such equal proportions.
